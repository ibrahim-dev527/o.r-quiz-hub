What is Operations Research?

Operations Research (OR) is a discipline that applies advanced analytical methods to help make better decisions. It uses mathematical modeling, statistical analysis, and optimization techniques to solve complex problems in business, engineering, logistics, and public policy. This platform focuses on developing your practical problem-solving abilities through targeted assessment.

Course Structure

Our curriculum is organized into six core modules that build upon each other, providing a comprehensive understanding of Operations Research principles and applications. Each module contains specialized quizzes designed to reinforce key concepts and develop problem-solving proficiency.

1Foundational Concepts

Introduction to optimization principles, model formulation, and problem-solving frameworks that underpin all Operations Research techniques.

2Inventory Management

Covers stock control models including Economic Order Quantity (EOQ), safety stock calculations, and inventory optimization strategies.

3Queuing Systems

Analysis of waiting line models, service rate analysis, queue discipline, and performance metrics for various queuing configurations.

4Optimization Methods

Linear programming techniques, simplex algorithm applications, transportation problems, and assignment models for resource allocation.

5Network Analysis

Project management methodologies including Critical Path Method (CPM), PERT analysis, and network optimization techniques.

6Advanced Applications

Integration of multiple techniques to solve complex real-world problems with practical case studies and comprehensive assessments.

Module Breakdown

Each module contains specialized quizzes focused on conceptual understanding and practical application. Questions are designed to develop analytical thinking and quantitative problem-solving skills essential for academic success.

Stock Control

Conceptual Focus

Inventory management models, cost minimization strategies, and optimal ordering policies under various constraints and demand patterns.

Quiz Expectations

Calculation-based questions on EOQ, safety stock levels, reorder points, and total inventory cost analysis with realistic scenarios.

Preparation Tips

  • Review basic algebra and calculus concepts
  • Understand cost components in inventory systems
  • Practice interpreting word problems into mathematical models

Queuing Theory

Conceptual Focus

Waiting line characteristics, service rate analysis, queue discipline, and performance evaluation metrics for single and multi-server systems.

Quiz Expectations

Problems involving arrival rates, service times, utilization factors, and calculations of key performance indicators like average wait time.

Preparation Tips

  • Understand probability distributions (Poisson, exponential)
  • Memorize key formulas for different queuing models
  • Practice unit conversions and rate calculations

Linear Programming

Conceptual Focus

Formulation of optimization problems, constraint analysis, feasible region identification, and solution interpretation using graphical and algebraic methods.

Quiz Expectations

Model formulation exercises, graphical solution interpretation, simplex method applications, and sensitivity analysis questions.

Preparation Tips

  • Practice converting word problems to mathematical models
  • Review graphical solution techniques
  • Understand slack/surplus variables and shadow prices

Transportation

Conceptual Focus

Optimization of distribution networks, cost minimization in shipping scenarios, and solution methods for balanced and unbalanced transportation problems.

Quiz Expectations

Problems requiring application of Northwest Corner, Vogel's Approximation, and MODI methods with step-by-step solution requirements.

Preparation Tips

  • Master initial solution techniques
  • Practice optimality testing procedures
  • Understand degeneracy handling in transportation tables

Assignment

Conceptual Focus

Optimal resource allocation problems, one-to-one assignment scenarios, and solution techniques for minimization and maximization objectives.

Quiz Expectations

Problems requiring application of the Hungarian algorithm with various constraint modifications and special cases.

Preparation Tips

  • Practice matrix reduction techniques
  • Understand handling of unbalanced assignment problems
  • Review maximization problem conversions

Project Management

Conceptual Focus

Network diagram construction, critical path identification, time-cost tradeoffs, and resource allocation in project scheduling contexts.

Quiz Expectations

Network diagram interpretation, forward/backward pass calculations, slack time determination, and project duration optimization problems.

Preparation Tips

  • Practice drawing network diagrams from activity lists
  • Master critical path calculations
  • Understand PERT time estimates and probability calculations
